Obsidian Power Stone Ring

Energy expressed as miniature purple branches radiating from the equator. Branches grow longer, wider, and brighter with intensity — and shrink, thin, and dim when decreasing. All contained within the obsidian body.

PHASE 79.1 - VISION v4 (BRANCHES)

Core Concept: Energy Branches

  • Opaque HD Obsidian — Solid polished volcanic obsidian. Mirror-like reflectivity. You cannot see through it.
  • Clear "O" Center — The torus hole is always open. It IS the Opta "O". Nothing fills it.
  • Branch-Based Energy — Instead of a smooth gradient, energy manifests as miniature purple branches/veins that sprout from the equator. They are abstract, organic, and asymmetric — not uniform rays.
  • Tri-Axis Response — When intensity rises, branches simultaneously get longer (extend further toward walls), wider (thicker stroke), and brighter (higher emission). The inverse on decrease.
Key Difference from v3

The energy is NOT a smooth radial gradient/glow. It is composed of many individual branch-like tendrils that grow organically from the equator. Each branch has its own timing, length, and slight randomness. The combined effect is abstract and alive, never a uniform ring of light.

Branch Behavior: Intensity Scaling

Each branch responds to intensity with three simultaneous changes. The animation is non-linear and organic — branches don't all grow at the same rate.

Low Short, thin, dim branches barely visible
Medium Wider, reaching halfway to walls, visible glow
Max Long, thick, bright — nearly touching both walls

Cross-Section: Branch Distribution

In the tube cross-section, branches radiate from the equator (midpoint) toward both walls. They are not uniform — some are longer, some shorter, creating an organic pattern.

Equator Outer O

Organic Branch Pattern

Origin: All branches sprout from the equator circle (midpoint of tube wall, radius 115px).

Direction: Each branch extends radially — some toward the outer wall, some toward the inner wall. Symmetrically distributed overall.

Variation: Individual branches vary in length, width, and timing. No two branches are identical. The pattern is abstract and organic, not geometric.

Containment: Branches never extend beyond the obsidian surface. They are subsurface energy veins visible through the polished stone.

The "O" - Always Clear

Center hole remains void

Branches grow toward the inner wall but never cross into the center hole. The "O" is always empty, defined by the surrounding obsidian and its internal energy veins. At maximum intensity, branches nearly touch the inner surface but the hole stays pure void.

Animation Principle

The intensity oscillates non-linearly. Branches don't grow/shrink uniformly — each has a phase offset (0-2π) and slightly different speed. Some branches lead, others lag. The combined effect is organic breathing, never mechanical pulsing.

Shader Strategy

Branches are generated procedurally in the fragment shader using noise-based patterns anchored to the equator distance field.

Generation
Noise-Based Branching
Use fractal Brownian motion (fBm) seeded by the angular position around the torus. Radial extent is masked by distance-from-equator. Each "branch" is a high-value corridor in the noise field.
branch = fbm(angle * freq) * radial_mask
Scaling
Tri-Axis Intensity Map
The uniform intensity drives three outputs simultaneously: noise threshold (reveals more branches), radial mask extent (longer reach), and emission multiplier (brighter glow).
length, width, brightness = f(intensity)
Surface
PBR + Subsurface Branch Emission
Standard Cook-Torrance obsidian on top. Branch emission blended underneath: final = reflection + branch_emission * (1 - fresnel). Branches most visible at perpendicular viewing angles.
Opaque output | No transmission
Noise Parameters

fbm(octaves=4, persistence=0.5, lacunarity=2.0) sampled at the fragment's angular position on the torus. The noise output is thresholded: values above 1.0 - intensity become branch pixels. As intensity rises, the threshold drops, revealing more and longer branches.
