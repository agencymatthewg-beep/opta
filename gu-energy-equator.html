Core Principles

3D Depth

The plasma exists in true 3D space — centered within the hollow tube cavity, not painted on the surface.

Symmetric Positioning

Equidistant from both the inner and outer walls of the torus. Follows the toroidal path around the ring.

Abstract & Variable

NOT a uniform gradient. Organic turbulence creates random bright spots and dim regions.

Alive, Not Mechanical

Feels like contained lightning or nebula gas — unpredictable, powerful, ready to escape.

Hot Spots

Random areas of intense brightness that appear and fade. 15% probability per frame, 0.92 decay rate.

High Turbulence

6 octaves of noise with aggressive domain warping. Chaos, not calm.

Technical Implementation

// Energy Equator positioning (shader pseudocode) tubeRadius = 0.35; // Radius of tube cross-section plasmaRadius = 0.0; // Plasma at CENTER (equator) plasmaThickness = 0.15; // How thick the band appears // Distance from tube center determines visibility distanceFromEquator = abs(localTubePosition - plasmaRadius); equatorFalloff = smoothstep(plasmaThickness, 0.0, distanceFromEquator); // Final plasma is brightest at equator, fades toward walls finalPlasmaColor = plasmaColor * equatorFalloff * plasmaIntensity;
// Hot spot generation hotSpotNoise = noise(position * 2.0 + time * 0.1); isHotSpot = hotSpotNoise > (1.0 - hotSpotProbability); plasmaIntensity = mix(basePlasma, 1.0, isHotSpot * hotSpotIntensity); // Noise parameters for high turbulence octaves = 6; lacunarity = 2.2; gain = 0.55; turbulenceScale = 3.0; turbulenceSpeed = 0.8;
